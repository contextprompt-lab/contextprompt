TL;DR

Read.ai is a sophisticated meeting intelligence platform — engagement scores, talk-time analytics, meeting quality reports, and video highlights that help managers understand how meetings run. contextprompt is purpose-built for engineering teams: it scans your repos, extracts structured coding tasks from the transcript, and maps them to real files in your codebase. If your sprint planning sessions need to produce Jira tickets instead of meeting health scores, contextprompt is the better fit.

Where contextprompt pulls ahead

Read.ai tells you how a meeting went. contextprompt tells you what to build from it.

📁

Repo-aware task extraction

contextprompt scans your connected repositories before processing the transcript — file paths, exported functions, component names. When your team says "fix the auth middleware," it maps that to src/middleware/auth.ts automatically. Read.ai sees generic text.

🤖

Output ready for AI coding agents

Every task contextprompt extracts is formatted with file references, implementation steps, and transcript evidence — exactly what Claude Code, Cursor, or Copilot needs to start coding. Read.ai outputs meeting reports; contextprompt outputs engineering work.

💰

Per-account pricing, not per-seat

contextprompt Pro is $30/month for your whole team. Read.ai charges $19.75 per user per month on annual plans — for a 5-person team that's $99/month vs $30/month. The gap widens as your team grows.

📊

Read.ai wins: meeting analytics

Read.ai's engagement scoring, talk-time breakdown, and meeting quality reports are genuinely useful for engineering managers who run a lot of 1-on-1s or team syncs and want to understand meeting dynamics — something contextprompt doesn't address.

🎬

Read.ai wins: video intelligence

Read.ai's Enterprise tier lets you create video highlights and jump to specific moments in recordings. If your team does async video reviews or needs to share specific meeting clips with stakeholders, Read.ai handles this natively.

🎯

contextprompt wins: task confidence

contextprompt rates each extracted coding task as high, medium, or low confidence — based on how clearly it was discussed and how precisely it maps to your repo. Read.ai lists action items without signal on which ones are well-defined enough to act on.
