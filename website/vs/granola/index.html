contextprompt vs Granola
for engineering teams

Granola is a beloved AI notepad that enhances your hand-typed meeting notes. contextprompt is a bot that joins your call and extracts structured coding tasks mapped to your actual codebase.

TL;DR

Granola is an excellent AI notepad for personal productivity — it listens to your meetings and enhances the notes you type, producing beautiful summaries. It runs locally on Mac, is fast, and has a polished UX. contextprompt does something different: it's a cloud bot that joins your meeting, scans your repos, and produces structured coding tasks with implementation steps and real file paths. If you want better notes, Granola is great. If you want to go from meeting to git commit, pick contextprompt.

Different tools, different jobs

Granola makes you a better note-taker. contextprompt eliminates the note-taking step entirely for engineering teams.

🔧

contextprompt: zero personal setup

Paste a meeting URL. A bot joins and records. You don't install anything, configure audio drivers, or even attend if the meeting is async. Granola requires a Mac app running on your machine during the call.

🗂️

contextprompt: knows your codebase

contextprompt connects to your repos before the meeting. After the call, it maps every discussed feature or bug to actual file paths, exports, and function signatures in your code. Granola knows nothing about your repo.

💻

contextprompt: any OS, any machine

Because contextprompt is cloud-based, it works on Windows, Linux, and Mac. It works when you're on the go, in a browser-only environment, or when a teammate needs to review tasks from a different machine.

📝

Granola wins: note-taking UX

Granola's core loop — type bullet notes while listening, AI enhances them into a polished document — is genuinely excellent. If you value your own notes and want AI to make them better, Granola's UX is superior.

🔒

Granola wins: local privacy

Granola processes audio locally on your device. For teams with strict data-handling requirements, not sending audio to the cloud is a real advantage. contextprompt sends transcripts to its cloud for analysis.

🌍

contextprompt wins: team history

All meetings and extracted tasks live in the contextprompt dashboard, accessible to anyone on the team. Past tasks link back to the transcript moment that created them. Granola's sharing is more personal-notebook oriented.

Which tool fits you

Both are great products. The choice comes down to what "output" means to you.

Choose contextprompt if you…

You want shipping tasks

  • Your meetings end with: "we need to change X file"
  • You use Claude Code, Cursor, or Copilot to implement
  • You want team-shared task history from every meeting
  • You run on Windows or Linux (Granola is Mac-only)
  • You don't want to install anything on your computer
  • You do sprint planning, retros, or architecture reviews
Choose Granola if you…

You want better notes

  • You like taking your own notes and want AI to polish them
  • You're on a Mac and prefer a native app experience
  • Data privacy is critical and you want local processing
  • You attend many varied meeting types (not just eng standups)
  • You want a personal AI assistant for all your calls
