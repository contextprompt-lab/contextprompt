contextprompt vs Tactiq
for engineering teams

Tactiq is a lightweight browser extension that transcribes your meetings in real time. If your meetings produce code rather than generic notes, there's a tool built for exactly that.

TL;DR

Tactiq is a fast, lightweight Chrome extension for live meeting transcription in 60+ languages — no bot, no audio driver, just real-time captions overlaid on your screen. It's great for quick transcripts and compliance-sensitive orgs. contextprompt is purpose-built for engineering teams: it sends a bot to your meeting, scans your connected repos, and outputs structured coding tasks mapped to real file paths. If you need "transcript now" Tactiq delivers. If you need "what do we build next and in which files," contextprompt is the answer.

Where contextprompt pulls ahead

Tactiq gives you a transcript. contextprompt gives you a sprint backlog item with file paths.

📁

Your repo is part of the analysis

contextprompt scans your connected repositories before analyzing the transcript. When your team says "refactor the database layer," it finds src/server/db.ts automatically. Tactiq outputs the same words everyone said — your codebase doesn't enter the picture.

🤖

Output designed for AI coding agents

Every extracted task includes the file paths, implementation steps, and transcript evidence that an AI coding assistant needs. Paste into Claude Code, Cursor, or Copilot and start implementing immediately. Tactiq outputs a text transcript you still have to parse yourself.

💾

Meeting recordings are stored

contextprompt stores your meeting audio and transcript for later review and reprocessing. Tactiq captures transcript-only — no recording. If you missed a standup, contextprompt has the whole audio. Tactiq has nothing unless you were in the tab.

💰

Flat account pricing — not per seat

contextprompt is $30/mo for your whole engineering team. Tactiq charges $12-20 per user per month. A 5-person team pays $30 vs $60-100. The savings compound as your team grows.

⚡

Tactiq wins: real-time captions

Tactiq's browser extension overlays live captions during your meeting — no waiting for a bot to join, no post-processing delay. Great for note-taking in real time and for participants with hearing needs. contextprompt is post-meeting only.

🔒

Tactiq wins: no bot in the room

Some clients and partners are uncomfortable with AI bots appearing in meetings. Tactiq's extension works invisibly in your browser — no bot participant shows up. contextprompt requires a Recall.ai bot that appears in the attendee list.

Who should pick which

Pick the tool that matches what happens after the meeting ends.

Choose contextprompt if you…

You ship software

  • Sprint planning or architecture meetings → coding tasks
  • Use Claude Code, Cursor, GitHub Copilot, or similar
  • Need tasks that reference real files, not "the auth thing"
  • Run engineering standups, design reviews, or retros
  • Want flat-rate pricing for your whole team
  • Need meeting recordings stored for async review
Choose Tactiq if you…

You want a lightweight transcript

  • Need real-time captions during the meeting itself
  • Can't or won't have a bot appear in your meetings
  • Work in a compliance-sensitive org (HIPAA, SOC 2)
  • Primarily need international language support (60+)
  • Want a free tier with no recording time limits
  • Don't need coding task extraction or repo scanning
