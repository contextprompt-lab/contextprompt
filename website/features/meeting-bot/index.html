What our meeting bot does

Most meeting bots stop at transcription. contextprompt's bot understands your codebase.

01

Joins as a named participant

Paste the meeting link in the dashboard. The bot joins within seconds as "contextprompt" — no calendar integration, no browser extension, no desktop client. Works on Zoom, Google Meet, Teams, and Slack Huddles.

02

Records with speaker attribution

The bot captures the full meeting audio and generates a transcript with speaker labels. Every statement is attributed to the right person — essential for accurate task ownership and meeting context.

03

Extracts tasks mapped to your code

When the call ends, contextprompt cross-references the transcript against your connected repos. It identifies every coding task discussed and maps each one to real file paths, function names, and implementation steps — not generic summaries.

Meeting bot FAQ

Common questions about how the meeting bot works.

How does the contextprompt meeting bot join my call?

Paste your meeting link into the contextprompt dashboard and click Send. The bot is dispatched automatically and joins your meeting as a participant named "contextprompt" — no calendar integration, no browser extension, no desktop app required.

Which meeting platforms are supported?

Zoom, Google Meet, Microsoft Teams, and Slack Huddles are all supported on both free and Pro plans. Paste any of these meeting links into contextprompt and the bot will join.

How is this different from other meeting bots like Otter or Fireflies?

Otter, Fireflies, and similar tools transcribe meetings and generate generic summaries or action item lists. contextprompt scans your connected code repos and maps each task back to real file paths, function names, and implementation steps — output specifically structured for AI coding assistants. See the AI meeting assistant for developers page for a full comparison.

Can I use this as a meeting bot API for my own app?

contextprompt is a ready-to-use product for engineering teams, not a raw meeting bot API. If you're building an application that needs bot-in-meeting infrastructure, check out Recall.ai or Meeting BaaS. If your goal is extracting repo-aware coding tasks from your team's meetings, contextprompt is the fastest path there — no API integration needed.

Is my meeting recording stored permanently?

Meeting audio is processed to generate the transcript and task output, then deleted per our data retention policy. Your meeting content is never used to train AI models. Only the extracted tasks and transcript are stored in your contextprompt account.
