Where contextprompt pulls ahead

tl;dv is excellent at preserving meeting knowledge. contextprompt is built to turn that knowledge into shipping velocity.

📁

Your codebase is in the loop

contextprompt scans your connected repos — file paths, exports, function signatures — before analyzing the transcript. When your team says "refactor the auth middleware," contextprompt finds src/middleware/auth.ts and includes it in the task automatically.

🤖

Output built for AI coding agents

Every extracted task includes what to change, which files to touch, and the transcript evidence behind the decision. Paste directly into Claude Code or Cursor and start implementing. tl;dv gives you clippable video moments — great for context, not for code.

🎯

Confidence scoring per task

contextprompt rates each task high, medium, or low confidence based on how explicitly it was discussed and how well it maps to your repo. You know which tasks are certain commits and which need clarification before you open your editor.

🎬

tl;dv wins: video + async teams

tl;dv's clip-and-share workflow is excellent for remote and async organizations. Creating timestamped highlights that stakeholders can watch later is a genuinely useful capability — particularly for sales, CS, and product teams reviewing customer calls.

🌍

tl;dv wins: multi-language

With transcription in 30+ languages, tl;dv is the better choice for international or multilingual engineering teams. contextprompt's repo-scanning and task extraction is optimized for English-language technical discussions.

💰

contextprompt wins: team pricing

tl;dv charges $18 per user per month on Pro. For a 5-person engineering team, that's $90/mo. contextprompt is $30/mo for the whole account — unlimited seats, unlimited meeting bots. The pricing scales with you, not against you.
