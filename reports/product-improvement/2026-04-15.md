## Problem

`PlanSelection.tsx` is the plan selection screen every new user hits immediately after signing in. Despite being a critical conversion gate (free → $30/mo Pro), it was generic and missing key conversion elements:

- **Headline:** "Choose your plan" — no product context, no momentum
- **Usage limits not contextualized:** "1 hour/month" and "15 hours/month" mean nothing to someone who doesn't know how long their meetings are
- **Weak Pro copy:** "For teams that ship fast" is vague; doesn't target the actual user (developer with regular standups/planning meetings)
- **No social proof** at the payment decision point
- **No trust signals** around the Stripe checkout flow
- **CTA copy:** "Start Pro" is ambiguous — doesn't feel like an upgrade

## Change

Updated `website/app/src/pages/PlanSelection.tsx` (27 lines added, 13 removed):

1. **Headline:** "Start turning meetings into code" — continues the product narrative, shows users they're in the right place
2. **Subtitle:** describes the full product loop (paste link → bot joins → transcribes → scans repos → extracts tasks) so users know exactly what they're activating before choosing a plan
3. **Trust badge line:** "No API keys needed · No credit card for free plan · Cancel anytime" — answers the three most common objections before the user even reads the cards
4. **Free features:** `1 hour/month (~2 meetings)` — concrete, not abstract
5. **Pro features:** `15 hours/month (~30 meetings)` — the 15× gap is now immediately legible
6. **Pro description:** "For developers and teams with regular standups, planning, or review meetings" — self-qualifying; users recognize themselves
7. **Pro CTA:** "Upgrade to Pro" — clearer intent than "Start Pro"
8. **Social proof quote:** Marcus R., Engineering Lead (reused from homepage testimonials — consistency across the funnel). Quote addresses the exact use case that drives Pro upgrades: standup → ticket creation workflow
9. **Trust signals footer:** "Secure checkout via Stripe · Cancel anytime · Instant activation" — removes payment hesitation

## Rationale

Conversion funnel state as of this run:
- **Login.tsx** improved 2026-04-01 (value props, "free to start" signal)
- **Recording.tsx** improved 2026-04-08 (first-meeting empty state)
- **PlanSelection.tsx** was the remaining uninstrumented gap before the user commits to a tier

The plan selection screen is the only moment in the funnel where a user is simultaneously:
1. Already committed (they authenticated with Google)
2. Undecided on price

That makes it the highest-leverage place to add social proof and remove uncertainty. Every friction point removed here directly affects MRR.
