contextprompt vs Tactiq
for engineering teams

Tactiq shows you a live transcript in your browser during meetings. contextprompt takes the conversation further — it scans your repos and turns every technical decision into a coding task with the exact file paths your team needs.

TL;DR

Tactiq is a lightweight Chrome extension that gives you live transcripts across Zoom, Google Meet, and Teams — extremely convenient for personal note-taking in 60+ languages. contextprompt operates at a different level: a bot joins your meeting, transcribes it, then scans your repos and uses Claude AI to extract structured coding tasks with real file paths. If your goal is meeting notes, Tactiq is fine. If your goal is shipping faster, contextprompt is the tool.

Where contextprompt pulls ahead

Tactiq captures your conversations. contextprompt turns them into your next sprint.

📁

Repo-aware task extraction

contextprompt scans your connected repos before analyzing the transcript. When your standup mentions "fix the webhook handler," contextprompt finds src/routes/webhooks.ts automatically — no manual guessing about which file to open.

🤖

Structured output for AI agents

Every extracted task includes what to implement, which files to touch, the implementation approach, and the discussion that motivated it. Drop the output directly into Claude Code and ship. Tactiq gives you a text transcript — useful raw material, not a ready-to-execute plan.

🚫

No browser extension required

contextprompt uses a bot that joins your meeting — just paste the URL. You don't need to install or maintain a Chrome extension. Tactiq requires the extension to be installed and active to capture audio, which adds setup friction especially across distributed teams.

⚡

Tactiq wins: live in-meeting view

Tactiq's real-time transcript appears in your browser alongside the meeting. If your team references the transcript during the call — checking what was said 5 minutes ago, reading back a decision — Tactiq's in-browser live feed is genuinely useful.

🌍

Tactiq wins: 60+ languages

Tactiq supports transcription in over 60 languages, making it a better fit for multilingual teams. If your engineering team conducts meetings in languages other than English, Tactiq's language coverage is a clear advantage.

🏗️

contextprompt wins: the full pipeline

Tactiq stops at transcription. contextprompt is an end-to-end pipeline: meeting bot joins → transcript + repo scan → Claude AI extraction → structured tasks ready for your dev tools. The gap between "transcript" and "ready-to-implement tasks" is where engineering teams lose hours every week.

Who should pick which

Tactiq and contextprompt both start from meetings — but they end at very different places.

Choose contextprompt if you…

You need tasks from meetings

  • Engineering standups or sprint planning → coding tasks
  • Use Claude Code, Cursor, GitHub Copilot, or similar
  • Need action items that reference actual source files
  • Prefer a bot that joins without browser extensions
  • Want flat per-account pricing as your team grows
  • Run GitHub issue analysis alongside meeting-driven work
Choose Tactiq if you…

You want live notes in-meeting

  • Need to see and reference transcripts during the meeting
  • Conduct meetings in languages other than English
  • Want a lightweight Chrome extension, not a full bot
  • Only need basic summaries and action items after the call
  • Have a tight budget (paid plans start at $8/user/mo)
