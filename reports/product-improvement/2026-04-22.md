## Problem

Free users who approach or exhaust their 1hr/month recording quota have no in-page signal that they're about to hit the limit, and no direct path to upgrade at the moment they need it.

The sidebar has a small progress bar showing "Recording: 45m / 60m" and a passive "Upgrade to Pro" chip — both visible at all times, regardless of usage level. This is too subtle: a user who has never thought about upgrading won't notice the chip, and a user at 90% usage sees the same passive chip as a user at 5%. There's no urgency, no copy explaining what happens when they hit the limit, and no contextual reason to act now.

When a free user actually hits 100% usage, new meetings silently fail — there's no explanation of why. This is the worst possible outcome: a user who wanted to pay wasn't asked to.

## Change

Added a contextual usage warning banner to `website/app/src/pages/Recording.tsx`:

- **At 75–99% usage:** Warning-level `Alert` with "15 min of recording left this month (45/60 min used). Upgrade to Pro for 15 hours/month ($30/mo)." + inline "Upgrade to Pro" button
- **At 100% usage:** Error-level `Alert` with "You've used all 60 min of your free plan this month — new meetings won't be recorded. Upgrade to Pro for 15 hours/month." + same button
- The button calls `createCheckoutSession()` and redirects to Stripe — same flow used by the sidebar chip
- The banner is **invisible to Pro users** and **invisible to free users under 75%** (no noise for normal use)

## Why This Page, Why This Trigger

The Meetings (`/record`) page is where users come to start a recording. It's the highest-intent moment in the product loop — the user is actively trying to use the product. Showing the upgrade prompt here (rather than just in the sidebar) makes it actionable: they're already deciding whether to start a meeting, so "you have 15 min left" directly addresses their current decision.

75% was chosen as the trigger because:
- Under 75%: users aren't constrained, no urgency created
- 75–99%: user can still record but should know they're close (one long meeting could exhaust the limit)
- 100%: user needs to know their meeting won't be processed before they waste time sending the bot
