Where contextprompt pulls ahead

tl;dv captures what was said. contextprompt turns what was said into what needs to be built.

📁

Your repo is part of the analysis

contextprompt scans your connected repositories — file paths, exports, function signatures — before analyzing the transcript. When your team says "fix the auth middleware," contextprompt finds src/server/middleware/auth.ts automatically. tl;dv doesn't know your codebase exists.

🤖

Output designed for AI coding agents

Every extracted task is formatted with the context an AI coding assistant needs: what to change, which files, and the transcript evidence. Paste into Claude Code and start implementing immediately. tl;dv gives you video clips and CRM notes.

💰

Flat account pricing — not per seat

contextprompt charges $30/mo for your whole team. tl;dv charges $18-29 per user per month. For a 5-person engineering team, that's $30 vs $90-145 per month. The math is straightforward.

🎯

Confidence levels per task

contextprompt rates each extracted task high, medium, or low confidence based on how clearly it was discussed and how well it maps to your repo. tl;dv focuses on sales objections and deal stages, not engineering task clarity.

📹

tl;dv wins: video highlights

tl;dv's video clipping is industry-leading — you can create shareable highlight reels from any meeting moment. Great for async reviews and stakeholder updates. contextprompt focuses on text transcripts and task extraction, not video.

📊

tl;dv wins: multi-meeting intelligence

tl;dv can roll up insights across dozens of calls — trends, recurring topics, coaching patterns. If you're a VP of Sales analyzing 50 customer calls per week, tl;dv's analytics are unmatched. Engineers typically care about one meeting at a time.
