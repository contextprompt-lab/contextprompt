The key difference

Fireflies.ai gives you a searchable transcript. contextprompt gives you something you can actually ship.

🗂️

Repo-aware task extraction

contextprompt scans your connected repos before extracting tasks. When the meeting mentions "the auth middleware" or "the calendar component," contextprompt resolves that to actual file paths in your codebase — not guesses.

⚡

AI coding assistant output

Every task includes implementation steps and file paths formatted for Claude Code, Cursor, or Copilot. Paste the output and ship. Fireflies gives you a transcript — you still have to figure out what to code and where.

🔗

Transcript evidence on every task

Each extracted task cites the exact quote from the meeting that triggered it, with speaker attribution. No more wondering "did they actually say that?" or replaying recordings.

📊

Fireflies wins: CRM + sales workflows

Fireflies has deep integrations with Salesforce, HubSpot, and other CRMs. If your team's output is sales calls, not code commits, Fireflies is the better fit.

🔍

Fireflies wins: meeting search at scale

Fireflies has a mature full-text search across years of meeting history. If you need to dig through 500 past calls, Fireflies' search UX is more polished for that workflow.

💰

contextprompt wins: simpler pricing

contextprompt charges per account ($30/mo Pro), not per seat. A team of five using Fireflies Pro pays $90+/mo. contextprompt Pro is $30/mo regardless of team size.

Who should use which

Both tools are good. The right one depends on what your team ships.

Choose contextprompt if you…

Your team ships code

  • Meetings produce GitHub issues, PRs, or Jira tickets
  • You use Claude Code, Cursor, or Copilot to implement features
  • You want tasks that reference real files, not vague descriptions
  • You're tired of rewriting meeting notes into prompts
  • You run sprint planning, standups, or design reviews
  • Your team is 1–20 engineers
Choose Fireflies.ai if you…

Your team sells

  • Primary meeting type is sales calls or customer interviews
  • You need deep CRM sync (Salesforce, HubSpot, Pipedrive)
  • You want to search across 100s of past calls
  • Your team is mostly non-technical
  • You need Slack/Notion/Zapier automations on every meeting
