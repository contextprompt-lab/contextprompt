Where contextprompt pulls ahead

Fathom is great at feeding CRM systems. contextprompt is built to feed your codebase.

📁

Repo scanning changes everything

contextprompt scans your connected repositories before processing the transcript. When your team says "add rate limiting to the API," contextprompt finds src/server/middleware/rateLimit.ts and includes it in the task with the correct file path. Fathom has no concept of your codebase.

🤖

Tasks formatted for AI agents

Every task contextprompt extracts is structured for an AI coding assistant: what to implement, which files to touch, the implementation approach, and the transcript evidence. Paste it into Claude Code and skip the prompt engineering. Fathom's action items are plain prose.

🎯

Task confidence scoring

contextprompt assigns each task a confidence level (high/medium/low) based on how explicitly it was discussed and how clearly it maps to your repo. You always know which decisions were committed and which need a follow-up question before you start coding.

💼

Fathom wins: sales and CS teams

If your team runs customer calls, discovery sessions, or demos, Fathom's CRM sync and sales-framework templates are a genuinely compelling workflow. Automatic Salesforce and HubSpot updates after every call removes a major pain point for revenue teams.

💬

Fathom wins: conversational Q&A

Fathom's ChatGPT-style interface lets you ask questions about your meeting after the fact. "What did the client say about budget?" works well for non-technical meeting review. contextprompt's output is structured tasks, not a chat interface.

💰

contextprompt wins: engineering team value

Fathom charges per user on paid plans. A 5-person engineering team pays $70–95/mo. contextprompt is $30/mo for the whole account. For code-producing meetings — standups, sprint planning, design reviews — contextprompt gives engineering teams dramatically more value per dollar.
