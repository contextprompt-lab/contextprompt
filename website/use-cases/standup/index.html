Why standups drain engineering teams

The standup was supposed to be a quick sync. Somewhere it became the meeting that eats the morning.

//

Standup prep steals deep work time

Engineers interrupt their flow every morning to remember what they worked on, gather context from scattered Slack threads and PRs, and structure three sentences for the team. For a 10-person team that is 20–30 minutes of interrupted focus per day before the meeting even starts.

?!

Status updates vanish after the call

Everything shared in a standup lives only in memory and fragmented notes. Engineers who were heads-down miss the sync. PMs trying to track progress have no record. Blockers raised in Tuesday's standup get re-raised Thursday because no one logged them.

~>

No code context means no actionable follow-up

Generic status updates — "working on the auth refactor" — tell the team nothing about what file is changing, which function is broken, or what PR to review. Stand-ups in plain language disconnect from the actual codebase, so blockers stay abstract and help never arrives.

Common questions

Everything you need to know about running standups with contextprompt.

Does contextprompt work with async standups?

Yes. If your team runs async standups via a scheduled Slack huddle or a recorded Loom, you can point contextprompt at the recording or meeting link. The same transcript-to-summary pipeline applies.

How does it know which files are relevant?

contextprompt scans your connected repositories — respecting .gitignore — and builds a code map of your file tree, function signatures, and exports. When the standup mentions a component or service by name, the AI matches it to the real path in your codebase.

Can it summarize standups for multiple teams or projects?

Yes. You can connect multiple repositories and run separate standup summaries per team or project. Each summary is scoped to the repos you associate with that meeting.

Does the bot disrupt the standup?

No. The contextprompt bot joins as a silent participant — it does not speak, react, or interrupt. Participants see it listed as a meeting attendee, nothing more.

What platforms are supported?

contextprompt supports Zoom, Google Meet, Slack huddles, and Microsoft Teams — the four platforms that cover the vast majority of engineering standups.
